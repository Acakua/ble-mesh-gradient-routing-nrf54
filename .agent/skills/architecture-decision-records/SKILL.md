# Architecture Decision Records

## Use this skill when
- Making significant architectural decisions.
- Documenting technology choices and trade-offs.

## Core Concepts
An ADR captures:
- **Context**: Why the decision was needed.
- **Decision**: What was decided.
- **Consequences**: What happens as a result (Positive/Negative/Risks).

## Template (Standard ADR)
1. **Status**: Proposed, Accepted, Deprecated, Superseded.
2. **Context**: Background and drivers.
3. **Considered Options**: Alternative approaches with pros/cons.
4. **Decision**: The chosen path.
5. **Rationale**: Why this option?
6. **Consequences**: Impact on the system and team.

## Best Practices
- Write ADRs early.
- Keep them short (1-2 pages).
- Be honest about trade-offs.
- Don't change accepted ADRs; supersede them.
